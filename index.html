Why I built this

I'm a bench scientist who moved into regulatory and compliance — building diagnostic programs that pass the science and the inspection. When I started looking for my next role on the path to becoming a high-complexity CLIA laboratory director, I hit the same wall every scientist does.

The labs I most wanted to reach weren't advertising. They existed — certified, staffed, running real testing — but they were invisible to every job board. The information was public, sitting in federal registries, just impossible to actually use.

So I built the tool I needed: one that lists every lab that legally exists, straight from the source, with no middleman and no scraped data. I'm sharing it because I know I'm not the only scientist staring at the same wall.

05

Built on data you can defend.

How you source your outreach is part of your credibility. LabLine is designed so you never have to explain where a number came from.

✓

Public federal data only

Every record comes from a public-domain U.S. federal registry with a documented API. Nothing is scraped, purchased, or licensed — and LinkedIn-style contact scraping is deliberately excluded.

✓

Your pipeline stays with you

No account, no server, no tracking. Your search, your notes, and your call statuses live in your own browser and are never transmitted anywhere.

✓

Facility lines, not people

LabLine surfaces business main lines only. It never stores or exports the personal contact data present in some source files. Manual dialing only — no autodialing.

Get on the list.

Be the first to know when new labs are certified in your state — and when new sources and features ship. Public-data updates and product news only.

No spam, no sharing your address. Opens your email app to send — nothing is collected by a server.
