**I built a free tool that lists every clinical lab + diagnostics firm in your state, so you can find the ones that aren't advertising jobs**

Job boards only show you roles a company chose to post. For a scientist targeting a clinical-lab,
diagnostics, or biotech career, that's a sliver of the real market — most labs never advertise.

**LabLine** pulls three public federal registries (CMS CLIA labs, NPPES lab organizations, and
FDA-registered device/biotech firms) into one searchable list by state — with phone numbers —
so you can reach labs directly. Filter to high-complexity/accredited labs, track who you called,
and export your pipeline. It runs entirely in your browser: no account, no server, no tracking,
and **nothing is scraped** — it's all public-domain federal data.

There's also an optional local-AI layer: connect your own Ollama model and it drafts tailored
cold-call talking points (grounded in web search, with guardrails — company facts only, no
personal dossiers). That part stays 100% on your machine.

It's open-source and free: **https://atito1234.github.io/labline/**

Built by a Ph.D. molecular geneticist who got tired of sending applications into the void.
Feedback and contributions welcome — especially new public data sources.
